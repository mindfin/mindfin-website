Types of Car Loans

New Car Loan

A new vehicle loan can be used to buy a brand new car right out of the showroom, as the name implies. Banks sell these kinds of loans with a variety of interest rates and loan terms.

Used Car Loan

Banks sell these kinds of loans up to 80%-85% of the car's value at a cost of 12-18% p.a. for a different loan period.

Car Refinance

When a person is in need of money, he or she will use his or her old vehicle as leverage to borrow enough money to buy a new car. This is referred to as car finance.

Basic Building Blocks for a Car loan.

1. Loan Cost.
An auto loan rate or expense is divided into two parts: the principal and the interest. The primary consideration is the vehicle's agreed price. The cumulative amount of costs incurred over the life of the loan depending on the principal amount and the given interest rate is referred to as interest.
2. Interest Rate.
A basic rate paid to the borrower for the money loaned is known as an interest rate. The annual percentage rate (APR) is the interest rate calculated as a percentage over a one-year term.
3. Down Payment.
The down payment is a lump sum payment made by the creditor at the time the car is purchased. It's normally expressed as a percentage of the overall cost. It is not a legal necessity when applying, but the lender almost always needs it.
4. Terms & Conditions.
This applies to all of the other components, such as the loan term, which is typically expressed in months or years, insurance and registration requirements, loan payoff and resale terms; ground conditions, theft and injury conditions, and mortgage default and repossession conditions.
