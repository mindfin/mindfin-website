Basic Functional Features of Home Loan

1. Disbursement of funds
When a borrower takes out a loan, the funds are used to buy the house. The creditor does not have access to the loan number.
2. Partial financing
Typically, financial institutions can fund up to 75% of the property as a loan. The borrower/buyer is responsible for the remainder.
3. Loan amount
Depending on the borrower's eligibility, the lender can give a loan of less than 75% of the property value. It's important to remember that you'll be approved for a home loan based on your eligibility, not the value of the property you want to buy.
4. Property mortgage
A financial institution that offers a lending scheme essentially mortgages a purchased property before the loan is paid off. This could be for 20 years or until the loan is fully paid off. In the event that the borrower defaults on the loan, the lender retains the right to sell the asset.

The Repayment Method

In most cases, the loan is repaid in equal monthly installments with post-dated checks. Banks typically require 12, 24, or 36 PDCS, in which you must repeat the process until the loan is paid off. To ensure timely replenishment of PDCS for the next 12, 24, or 36 months, some banks may require a check for an amount equal to the loan outstanding at the end of the PDC duration. If your installments are to be deducted from your paycheck. You'll need a letter from your boss approving this agreement and directing the funds to your bank account on a monthly basis. This is only possible if the company has made a deal with the bank.

Few banks allow you to order the bank where you have your savings current account to subtract money from your savings account each month and credit your home mortgage account.

Some banks allow you to pay your monthly installments using an ECS facility. Cash or demand draught are two other options for payment (not all banks offer this). Every month, you can deposit the EMI at the bank's office.
