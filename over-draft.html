OVER DRAFT

Banks provide an overdraft facility as a financial service with an OD account. An overdraft is a credit line provided by a lender when a customer's account balance falls below zero. The overdraft allows the account holder to withdraw money even if there are no funds in the account or insufficient cash to pay the withdrawal amount. When you open a bank account, you gain access to a wide range of financial services. To help you handle and maintain your deposits, the bank issues a cheque and a passbook. You'll also get an ATM/debit card, as well as access to the internet and mobile banking. What is an overdraft facility, though? Here's what you need to understand.

An overdraft is a financial instrument that allows money to be taken out of a current or savings account even though the balance falls below zero. It is a form of monetary limit extension provided by banks, and the money is referred to as being "overdrawn." Each customer is given an approved overdraft limit based on their relationship with the bank. The customer has the option of withdrawing money up to the stated amount. On money withdrawn in the form of an overdraft, banks charge interest.

Overdraft Facility Interest Rate

The interest rate on an od facility provided by private and public sector banks varies by lender and is determined by the appropriate loan amount, repayment term, and other factors. The interest rate is maintained at 1% over the time deposit rate. The repayment time is determined by the borrower's repayment capacity and the sum of the od.

What is an Overdraft and How Does It Work?

An overdraft is a type of credit provided to individuals by a financial institution and is linked to a bank account—usually a checking account. When a customer's account does not have enough money to make a transaction, the od covers the shortfall, causing the account to go into negative territory. It is a form of monetary limit extension provided by banks, and the money is termed to be an 'overdrawn account.'

Overdraft insurance is the method of awarding short-term credit to an account holder when their balance falls below zero. Overdraft insurance comes in a variety of shapes and sizes, and it works in a variety of ways depending on the banking relationship. Od security usually involves linking two accounts together, allowing funds to be immediately drawn on a backup account if the primary account falls below zero. This feature will help you prevent od fees or not having enough funds to complete a transaction.

Overdraft Limit

The bank's overdraft limit is the amount of money that can be withdrawn in addition to the credit bank balance. Furthermore, if a customer exceeds his or her overdraft limit or makes a failed payment, the bank would levy additional fees. Financial institutions also provide an OD Limit against the property.

Several banks and financial organizations offer od facilities that can be used to minimize the amount of EMI on your loans.
